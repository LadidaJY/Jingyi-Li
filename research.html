My Research

Sensory Information Constraind Trophic interactions

Understanding how the information flow between species affects their interactions is the main topic of my PhD thesis. I investigate how sensory information constrain species trophic interactions and influences predator-prey dynamics and species coexistence in changing environments.

more details...

Key Aspects

  • Quantifying information flow in predator-prey systems
  • Modeling sensory perception in complex environments
  • Assessing impacts of anthropogenic noise on ecological networks
  • Developing conservation strategies based on information theory

Trophic Interaction Inference

My research on trophic interaction inference focuses on developing novel methods to predict and analyze food web structures using limited observational data. This approach combines machine learning with ecological theory to reconstruct complex feeding networks.

By integrating trait-based modeling and network analysis, I aim to uncover hidden patterns in trophic interactions and predict how these networks respond to environmental changes such as climate change and habitat fragmentation.

Methodologies

  • Machine learning for interaction prediction
  • Stable isotope analysis of trophic positions
  • Network analysis of food web stability
  • Trait-based modeling of predator-prey interactions
